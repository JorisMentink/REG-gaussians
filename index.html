REG-Gaussians: 4D-Gaussian splatting for
sparse-view dynamic CBCT reconstruction
Joris Mentink1, Lequan Yu2, Kailing Wang2
1 Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e)
2 The University of Hong Kong (HKU)
Paper GitHub

Abstract

Iterative reconstruction algorithms have emerged as effective alternatives to conventional CBCT reconstruction methods, offering improved stability and image quality, even in sparse-view settings. This makes them well suited for radiation dose reduction in 4D-CBCT. Gaussian splatting has recently shown strong potential in this context, achieving higher reconstruction quality and training efficiency than neural radiance field-based approaches. This report presents REG-Gaussians, a 4D Gaussian splatting framework that extends prior 3DGS-based CBCT reconstruction and existing 4DGS methods. Three additional regularization strategies are investigated: spatiotemporal encoder grid smoothing, Gaussian geometric regularization, and motion coherence. Experimental results demonstrate that 4DGS can robustly reconstruct 4D CBCT volumes at low iteration counts and under extreme sparse-view conditions. Grid smoothing and motion coherence consistently improve reconstruction quality over the base model and, in some cases, state-of-the-art methods, while geometric regularization is found to be overly restrictive under the evaluated settings.
