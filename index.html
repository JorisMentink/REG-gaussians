REG-Gaussians

Regularized Gaussian representations for sparse-view and dynamic CT reconstruction.

Paper (PDF) Code Data Video

Abstract

REG-Gaussians explores the use of explicit regularization strategies in Gaussian splatting frameworks for sparse-view and dynamic CT reconstruction. By enforcing geometric consistency and temporal coherence, the proposed approach improves convergence stability and reconstruction quality compared to unregularized baselines.

Method

The method initializes a set of three-dimensional Gaussian primitives from an FDK reconstruction of sparsely sampled CBCT projections. These primitives are optimized through differentiable projection rendering using a compound loss function consisting of a photometric term and several regularization terms.

  • FDK-based initialization of Gaussian primitives
  • Differentiable forward projection
  • Spatiotemporal deformation modeling
  • Geometric and motion-based regularization

Results

The proposed regularization strategies were evaluated on the DIR-Lung 4DCT dataset under severe sparse-view conditions. Quantitative results demonstrate consistent improvements in PSNR and SSIM.
